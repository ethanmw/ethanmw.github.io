My Extracurriculars.

Band of the Fighting Irish

Music has always been one of my biggest passions outside the classroom. I began taking piano lessons in kindergarten, and in fifth grade I picked up the saxophone. I played saxophone through high school, where I achieved Arkansas All-State honors, and when I came to Notre Dame, I joined the Band of the Fighting Irish. Through the band, I've had some incredible experiences, including marching at the 2016 Battlefrog Fiesta Bowl, marching in a parade at Walt Disney World for the 2018 Citrus Bowl, playing in Fenway Park, and performing with the Boston Pops Orchestra in Boston Symphony Hall. For my junior year, I was selected to be a member of the Irish Guard, a group of skilled marchers (approximately a dozen in number) chosen on the basis of leadership, marching ability, and spirit. The Irish Guard wear traditional highland dress featuring the university's own Notre Dame Marching Guard tartan, perform the traditional Victory Clog to Damhsa Bua, and lead the band in march-outs and parades. I have also earned three "shamrock awards" for perfect attendance during the marching season, one for each year I have been a member of the marching band.

In addition to the marching band, I am a member of our basketball pep band and our Symphonic Band, one of the university's auditioned concert bands. I also play in our saxophone choir, an all-saxophone ensemble including soprano, alto, tenor, baritone, and bass saxes, and in which I hope to debut an arrangment of my own this spring.
